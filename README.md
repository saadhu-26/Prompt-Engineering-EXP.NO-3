## EX.NO:3 Exploring the different types of prompts- Straightforward Prompts - Tabular Format Prompting - Missing Word Prompting - Preceding Question Prompting.

## Aim: To write the prompts for the following prompt types for 2 different scenarios of Each type.

The primary aim of this experiment is to design an AI-powered chatbot that can assist customers in resolving issues related to product troubleshooting, order  tracking, and general inquiries. The chatbot should demonstrate efficiency, accuracy, and user-friendliness in handling multiple types of customer interactions.This experiment also aims to explore how different prompt engineering techniques influence the chatbot’s responses and improve customer satisfaction.

## Objectives:

 1. To understand the role of prompt engineering in chatbot development.
 2. To apply four different prompt types: Straightforward, Tabular, Missing Word, and Preceding Question.
 3. To analyze how the chatbot’s accuracy and clarity vary across prompt types.
 4. To develop an effective conversational flow suitable for real-world customer service.
 5. To recommend the most effective hybrid approach for building AI-powered chatbots.

## Procedure:

Step 1: Define the chatbot’s problem-solving scope including product troubleshooting, order tracking, and general inquiries.
Step 2: Identify suitable prompt engineering techniques based on query type andexpected outcome.
Step 3: Design customer queries for each prompt type and prepare sample responses.
Step 4: Implement chatbot responses using the chosen prompt engineering methods.
Step 5: Compare the outputs in terms of clarity, correctness, and user satisfaction.
Step 6: Record observations and summarize results with recommendations forreal-world deployment.

## Types of Prompt Engineering with Examples:

## 1. Straightforward Prompts
Definition: Direct instructions/questions without additional context.
Example:
Customer: “Track my order with order ID 56789.”
Chatbot: “Your order with ID 56789 is out for delivery and will arrive tomorrow.”
Use case: Best for quick queries like order status or warranty checks.

## 3. Missing Word Prompting
Definition: Prompts with blanks that the AI fills in with correct information.
Example:
Customer: “The warranty period for this laptop is ___ months.”
Chatbot: “The warranty period for this laptop is 12 months.”
Use case: Useful for factual completions like warranty details or delivery timelines.

## 4. Preceding Question Prompting
Definition: Asks an introductory/preparatory question before answering the main
query.
Example:
Chatbot: “Before I help you, can you confirm if your device has sufficient charge?
Now, what problem are you facing with your laptop?”
Customer: “Yes, it has charge, but it freezes frequently.”
Chatbot: “Thank you. Since it has charge, the freezing issue could be due to
software. Please try restarting or updating the system.”
Use case: Best for guided troubleshooting requiring step-by-step diagnosis.

## RESULT: 
From this experiment, we observed that different prompt types serve different purposes.Straightforward prompts are efficient for quick and simple tasks.Tabular prompts enhance readability and are excellent for technical troubleshooting.Missing word prompts are valuable when specific factual information is required,while preceding question prompts improve the chatbot’s ability to provide context-aware and step-by-step assistance. Combining these techniques results in a highly effective and user-friendly chatbot.

Conclusion: The hybrid approach, which strategically mixes all four prompt types,is the most powerful method for building AI-powered customer support chatbots.This approach ensures speed, accuracy, clarity, and context handling, making the chatbot adaptable to diverse customer needs
